refers to a job opening for a Clinical Officer—a trained healthcare professional who diagnoses and treats illnesses, performs medical procedures, and may assist in surgeries, depending on the country and specific regulations.

Typical Responsibilities of a Clinical Officer. Diagnosing and treating common illnesses and injuries, Prescribing medication and administering treatments, Performing minor surgical procedures (like suturing wounds or draining abscesses), Ordering and interpreting laboratory tests and imaging, Referring patients to specialists or hospitals for advanced care, Maintaining patient records and writing medical reports, Sometimes involved in maternal and child health, family planning, or managing HIV/AIDS or TB patients.

Common Requirements for Clinical Officer Vacancies: Diploma or Bachelor’s degree in Clinical Medicine from a recognized institution. Registration with the relevant Clinical Officers Council or regulatory body (e.g., Clinical Officers Council of Kenya). A valid practicing license. At least 1–3 years of working experience in a healthcare setting (though fresh graduates may also be considered in some positions). CPD (Continuous Professional Development) certifications or courses may be an added advantage.
